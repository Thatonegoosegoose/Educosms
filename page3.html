THE MILKY WAY

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our solar system. It is a huge spiral galaxy made of billions of stars, planets, gas, and dust. From Earth, it can be seen as a bright band of light across the night sky.
Galaxies are massive groups of stars, planets, gas, and dust held together by gravity. There are different types of galaxies, such as spiral, elliptical, and irregular. The Milky Way is one example of a galaxy.
Galaxies formed billions of years ago after the Big Bang. Gravity pulled gas and dust together, creating stars and star systems. Over time, these groups of stars grew into the galaxies we see today.
