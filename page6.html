THE BIG BANG

The universe began about 13.8 billion years ago in an extremely hot and dense state. Space itself started expanding, and energy and particles filled the young universe. Scientists call this event "The Big Bang".
As the universe expanded and cooled, particles joined to form atoms, then stars and galaxies. Over billions of years, gravity shaped these into the large cosmic structures we observe today.
In the far future, the universe may reach a state called heat death, where energy is evenly spread out. Stars burn out, motion slows, and the universe becomes cold, dark, and quiet.
