THE SPACE RACE

The Space Race was a competition between the United States and the Soviet Union during the Cold War to achieve dominance in space exploration. Both nations aimed to show their technological and scientific superiority.
It began in 1957 when the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite, surprising the world and sparking fear and excitement. This led to rapid advances in rocket technology and space programs in both countries.
The rivalry produced many milestones, including the first human in space, Yuri Gagarin, and the first Moon landing by the United States in 1969. The Space Race helped establish the foundation for modern space exploration.
