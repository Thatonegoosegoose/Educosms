BLACK HOLES

Black holes are mysterious regions in space where gravity is extremely strong, so strong that even light cannot escape. They usually form when very large stars collapse at the end of their lives.
A star begins as a cloud of gas and dust called a nebula. Gravity pulls the material together until the star ignites and shines for millions of years. When it runs out of fuel, it can become a red giant and eventually end as a white dwarf, neutron star, or black hole.
Black holes bend space like a deep dip in a trampoline. Objects that come too close slide into the dip and cannot escape.
